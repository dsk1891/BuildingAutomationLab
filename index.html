Building Automation Laboratory

Laboratory

A Building Automation Laboratory is a specialized environment for developing, testing, and simulating Building Automation and Control Systems (BACS). These facilities integrate HVAC, lighting, and security systems with IoT and data science to optimize energy efficiency, sustainability, and smart infrastructure operations

Chilled Water System (CWS)

A Chilled Water System (CWS) is a centralized HVAC setup that produces and circulates cold water to cool buildings or industrial equipment. It works via a refrigeration cycle by absorbing heat from a space, transferring it to the water, and rejecting that heat outside

Air Handling Unit (AHU)

An Air Handling Unit (AHU) is a large, central piece of equipment in an HVAC system that regulates and circulates air throughout a building. Often acting as the "heart" of the system, it draws in outside air, filters out pollutants, adjusts the temperature and humidity, and distributes it via ductwork

Building Management System (BMS)

A Building Management System (BMS) is a centralized, computer-based control system that automates, monitors, and manages a facility's mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) infrastructure. It functions as a building's digital nervous system, seamlessly integrating HVAC, lighting, security, and fire safety to maximize energy efficiency and occupant comfort.
